Ranunculus aconitifolius

Common name
Aconite-leaved Buttercup
Deutscher Name
Eisenhutblättriger Hahnenfuß (monkshood-leaved buttercup)
Family
Ranunculaceae
Family common name
Buttercup family
Blooms
April - E August
Habitat
Banks, meadows, and pastures. Damp, nutrient-rich soils between 600 m. (200 ft.) and 2500 m. (8000 ft).
Distinguishing traits:
R. aconitifolius R. platanifolius
~2 ft. tall ~4 ft. tall
Middle segment of basal leaf divided to the base Middle segment of basal leaf not divided to the base (connate 2,5-15 mm from the base)
upper cauline leaves wide, serrate-dentate upper cauline leaves narrow, almost entire
pedicels pubescent above, 1-3 times as long as subtending leaf pedicels glabrous or nearly glabrous above, 3-5 times as long as subtending leaf
stamens as long as carpels stamens longer than carpels
carpels 2-3 mm long carpels 3-4 mm long
