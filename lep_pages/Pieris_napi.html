Pieris napi

Common name
Green-veined White
Deutscher Name
Grünader-Weißling
Family
Pieridae
Family common name
Whites, Yellows, and Sulphurs
On the wing
March to October
One to four (us. 2-3) generations
Habitat
Everywhere, but especially woody places and in forest fringes.
Larval host:
Mostly wild Brassicaceae, such as Alliaria petiolata or Cardamine species. It occurs occasionally on Reseda and rarely also capuchin cress (Tropaeolum).
